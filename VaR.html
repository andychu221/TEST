Risk Methodology & Calculation Mechanics

1. EWMA Volatility-Updating Historical Simulation

The dashboard employs Hull's Volatility-Updating Historical Simulation model. Traditional historical simulation reacts slowly to sudden market shocks. By updating historical returns with current volatility, the model ensures that the simulated distribution reflects the current market environment.

  • Step 1: Calculate daily simple returns for all assets: Return(t) = P(t) / P(t-1) - 1.
  • Step 2: Estimate daily variance using the Exponentially Weighted Moving Average (EWMA) model. The variance for day t is updated as:
    σ²(t) = λ × σ²(t-1) + (1 - λ) × Return(t-1)²
    By default, λ = 0.94 (RiskMetrics standard).
  • Step 3: Scale the historical returns to reflect current market conditions:
    Scaled Return(i) = Historical Return(i) × (σ_current / σ_historical_i)

2. Fund VaR Calculation

For Mutual Funds, the uploaded NAV (Net Asset Value) directly represents the total Position in USD. Cashflows (Deposits and Withdrawals) adjust the internal position, but the primary risk factor is the NAV percentage change.

Calculation per historical scenario i:
Simulated PnL(i) = -1 × Position(current) × Scaled Return(i)
(A positive PnL denotes a loss. If the position is Long and Return is negative, it results in a Loss.)

To compute the standalone Fund VaR, we sort the Simulated PnL array descendingly and extract the 95th or 99th percentile value based on the Lookback Window.

3. FX Position Netting & Portfolio VaR (Crucial)

Managing FX risk requires rigorous Cash Flow Mapping and Netting. Holding foreign currency implies an underlying short position against the base currency (USD). The dashboard automatically isolates the pure USD exposure to accurately capture diversification benefits.

Example Scenario:

  • Uploaded USD Position: -500M (Short USD)
  • Uploaded EUR Position: +100M EUR (Long EUR vs USD)
  • Uploaded JPY Position: +50M JPY (Long JPY vs USD)

Being Long EUR means you are holding EUR and structurally Short USD. The system calculates the true underlying Net USD exposure before applying the simulation:

True Net USD Position = Raw USD (-500) + EUR equivalent in USD (+100) + JPY equivalent in USD (+50)
True Net USD Position = -350M USD

In the Total FX VaR calculation, the engine uses this -350M net exposure. If the USD appreciates, the Net USD position will experience losses/gains simultaneously with the EUR and JPY legs. Aggregating these PnLs across all assets before taking the percentile perfectly captures empirical correlation and Diversification Effect.
